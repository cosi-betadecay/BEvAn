# Observations and Galactic Morphology (Paper Sec II–III)

## Detection history (Sec II.B)

- **1972**: first detection of a celestial γ-ray line from the Galactic-center
  direction (Johnson et al., NaI balloon) — the first γ-ray line ever from outside
  the solar system.
- **1978**: resolved as **511.0 keV** (FWHM 3.2 keV) with a high-resolution **Ge**
  balloon detector (Leventhal et al.), plus the positronium continuum → ~92% of
  annihilations occur after Ps formation. (Note: this is the first time the
  high-resolution Ge — the COSI detector technology — pinned the line.)
- **1980s**: apparent flux variability from balloons + SIGMA/GRANAT transient line
  features (e.g. 1E 1740.7-2942, the "Great Annihilator" microquasar). SMM (large
  FOV) showed the steady emission was real; variability claims faded.
- **1990s — OSSE/CGRO**: first imaging; excluded a single point source; established
  an extended bulge + disk, with the surprising **bulge dominance**.
- **2000s — INTEGRAL/SPI**: the definitive maps (Knödlseder et al. 2005;
  Weidenspointner et al. 2006, 2008a). SPI: 3° angular resolution, ~2.1 keV
  spectral resolution (Ge), spatially-resolved spectroscopy.

## Morphology (Sec II.B.3, IV.D.3)

- The 511 keV emission is **strongly concentrated in the Galactic bulge**, with a
  weaker disk — a bulge/disk ratio **unlike any other wavelength** (Figs 2–4).
- Model fits (Table I, Weidenspointner et al. 2008a): a **narrow bulge** (FWHM 3°)
  + **broad bulge** (FWHM 11°) + a **thick disk** (FWHM 7°). Luminosity
  **B/D ≈ 1.4**; total Galactic e⁺ annihilation rate **≳ 2 × 10⁴³ s⁻¹**.
  Alternative halo+thin-disk models with B/D ~ 6 also fit (low-surface-brightness
  components are poorly constrained).
- A claimed **disk asymmetry** (negative-longitude flux ~80% brighter; matches the
  hard-LMXRB distribution, Weidenspointner et al. 2008a) — but not confirmed by
  other analyses (Bouchet et al.); needs more data.
- The bulge-dominated, B/D > 1 morphology is the single hardest constraint: no
  conventional source population (which are disk-like) reproduces it.

## Spectral analysis (Sec II.B.4) — see also positronium-and-spectrum.md

- Line at **511 keV** = narrow (FWHM 1.3 keV, ~2/3 flux) + broad (FWHM 5.4 keV,
  ~1/3 flux) Gaussians + ortho-Ps 3γ continuum.
- **f_Ps = 0.97 ± 0.02** → positrons annihilate cold, in warm (T ~ 8000 K) gas,
  roughly equal neutral + ionized; <8% cold molecular, <0.5% hot, <1.2% grains.

## The ²⁶Al 1.8 MeV connection (Sec II.C.2)

²⁶Al (β⁺, BR 0.82, τ = 7.4×10⁵ yr) emits a **1809 keV (1.8 MeV)** line on decay,
first detected by HEAO-3 (1982) and mapped over 9 years by **COMPTEL** (Fig 7):
clumpy, along the disk, brightest in the inner radian, aligned with spiral-arm
tangents (Cygnus stands out). SPI rate Ṅ₂₆ ≈ 4.3 × 10⁴² s⁻¹ (~2.7 M_⊙/Myr). The
1.8 MeV map is the cleanest tracer of massive-star nucleosynthesis and of the
disk positron production from ²⁶Al + ⁴⁴Ti. The MeV continuum of the inner Galaxy
(power-law index ~1.55) is mostly cosmic-ray e⁻/e⁺ bremsstrahlung + IC; it bounds
the in-flight annihilation contribution and hence the injection energy of
positrons.

## Galactic background material (Sec III) — context for ISM annihilation sites

- **Stellar populations**: Milky Way ~5 × 10¹⁰ M_⊙ in stars; thin disk (0–10 Gyr),
  thick disk (>10 Gyr), bulge (~1/3 of mass, old >10 Gyr, tri-axial/barred), halo
  (<1%), nuclear bulge (~1.5×10⁹ M_⊙ within 230 pc), SMBH ~4×10⁶ M_⊙. Sun at
  R_⊙ ≈ 8 kpc.
- **ISM phases** (Table IV) — sets where positrons annihilate:
  | Phase | T (K) | n_H (cm⁻³) | ionization |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Molecular (MM) | 10–20 | 10²–10⁶ | ≲10⁻⁴ |
  | Cold neutral (CNM) | 50–100 | 20–60 | ~10⁻³ |
  | Warm neutral (WNM) | 6000–10⁴ | 0.2–0.5 | ~0.01–0.05 |
  | Warm ionized (WIM) | ~8000 | 0.1–0.3 | 0.6–0.9 |
  | Hot ionized (HIM) | ~10⁶ | ~0.003–0.01 | 1 |
  Bulge gas is roughly equal neutral/ionized, ~90% of the neutral being molecular
  (the Central Molecular Zone). The spectral analysis says positrons die in the
  **warm** phases.
- **SFR / SN rates** (Table V): Galactic SFR ~2 M_⊙/yr; core-collapse SN ~2/century,
  SN Ia ~0.5/century; these set the radioactive-positron production.
- **Magnetic field** (Sec III.D): regular component B_u ~1.5 μG near the Sun
  (total few μG), rising toward the GC (≳ 50 μG in the inner bulge), plus a
  turbulent component (Kolmogorov-like spectrum). The field configuration governs
  positron propagation (see [propagation.md](propagation.md)).
